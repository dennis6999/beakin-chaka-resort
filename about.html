Our Story

Humble Beginnings

Founded in 2015 by Joseph Kamau, a lifelong Chaka resident who wanted to share the area's quiet beauty with travelers seeking authentic Kenyan experiences. What began as a simple guesthouse has grown into a beloved retreat while maintaining its intimate, family-run atmosphere.

The Beakin Difference

We're not just a stay—we're your gateway to authentic Chaka. Our partnerships with nearby farms and markets mean you'll taste the freshest local produce and experience the community in a way that's impossible from larger hotels.

Name Origin

"Beakin" comes from the Kikuyu word "mbeki," meaning "serene" or "peaceful"—a perfect description of our little corner of Chaka Town. The name reflects our commitment to creating a tranquil space where guests can truly unwind.

Behind Beakin Chaka

Joseph Kamau, Owner

"I built this resort to share the Chaka I love—slow, simple, and full of heart. After 15 years in corporate Nairobi, I returned to my farming roots and created a place where travelers could experience the authentic Kenya I grew up with."

Grace Wanjiku, Head Chef

"I've been cooking for Beakin Chaka since day one. My recipes come from generations of family tradition, and I take pride in serving dishes that tell the story of our region."

Peter Mwangi, Groundskeeper

Chaka Town Love

Why Chaka?

Unlike busier tourist hubs, Chaka moves at its own pace—where farmers greet you by name and stars outshine city lights. Our little town has preserved its authentic character while welcoming visitors who appreciate genuine experiences.

Local Pride

  • Home to Kenya's freshest milk from our local dairy farms
  • Unparalleled sunrise views of Mt. Kenya from our doorstep
  • Rich coffee heritage with family-owned farms dating back generations
  • A community where traditional values meet modern hospitality

Our Commitment to Sustainability

Solar Power

We've installed solar panels to reduce our carbon footprint and harness Kenya's abundant sunshine.

Water Conservation

Our rainwater harvesting system helps us maintain our gardens while respecting this precious resource.

Local Sourcing

We partner with nearby farms to source fresh, seasonal ingredients, supporting our local economy.

Waste Reduction

We've implemented recycling programs and encourage guests to join us in minimizing waste.

How You Can Help

During your stay, you can contribute to our sustainability efforts by:

  • Reusing towels to conserve water
  • Separating recyclables from general waste
  • Turning off lights and air conditioning when leaving your room
  • Participating in our "zero-waste" dining options

Come as Guests, Leave as Family

Experience the warmth of Kenyan hospitality in a setting that feels like home. At Beakin Chaka, you're not just booking a room—you're joining our extended family.
